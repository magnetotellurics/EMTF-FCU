Statistical estimates for MT Transfer Functions

Covariance: COV

 

The covariance between two random variables X and Y is defined as the expected product of their deviations from their individual expected values:

\mathbf{Cov}(X,Y)=E[(X-E[X])(Y-E[Y])}

where E[X] is the expected value of X, also known as the mean of X.

In magnetotellurics, the full error covariance is necessary to allow rotation of the error bars associated with the MT transfer functions (see Kelbert (2019) for a detailed discussion).

If the full error covariance is available, it is usually supplied in the form of two matrices: the inverse signal power $\mathbf{S}$ and the residual covariance matrix $\mathbf{N}$ (Eisel & Egbert, 2001). The full covariance is the Kronecker delta product of the two,

\mathbf{Cov}[\mathbf{Z}] = \mathbf{N} \otimes \mathbf{S},

so that

\mathbf{Cov}[Z_{ij} Z_{i' j'}] = N_{i i'} S_{j j'}, \;\;\; j,j' = 1,2 \;\;\;\mathtt{ and }\;\;\; i,i' = 1,\ldots,N,

where $N$ is the number of output channels. To compute the variance,

\sigma^2_{ij} = \mathbf{Var}[Z_{ij}] = \mathbf{Cov}[Z_{ij} Z_{ij}] = N_{i i} S_{j j}, \;\;\; j = 1,2 \;\;\;\mathtt{ and }\;\;\; i = 1,\ldots,N.

Upon rotation,

\mathbf{Cov}[\mathbf{Z}'] = \mathbf{N'} \otimes \mathbf{S'} = [\mathbf{V} \otimes \mathbf{U}] [\mathbf{N} \otimes \mathbf{S}] [\mathbf{V} \otimes \mathbf{U}]^T,

where matrices $\mathbf{U}$ and $\mathbf{V}$ are rotation matrices as described in detail in Kelbert (2109). Then,

\sigma'^2_{ij} = \mathbf{Var}[Z'_{ij}] = \mathbf{Cov}[Z'_{ij} Z'_{ij}] = N'_{i i} S'_{j j}, \;\;\; j = 1,2 \;\;\;\mathtt{ and }\;\;\; i = 1,\ldots,N.

The new inverse signal power $\mathbf{S'}$ and residual covariance matrix $\mathbf{N'}$ may thus be computed using the expressions $\mathbf{N'} = \mathbf{V} \mathbf{N} \mathbf{V}^T$ and $\mathbf{S'} = \mathbf{U} \mathbf{S} \mathbf{U}^T$, as in Eisel & Egbert (2001).

Full error covariances may also be obtained from cross-power spectra (Kelbert, 2019).
