Definitions for MT Transfer Functions and Derived Quantities

MT impedance phase: PHS

 

The impedance phases are given by:

\phi_{ij}=\mathrm{tan}^{-1} \bigg( \frac{Im(Z_{ij})}{Re(Z_{ij})} \bigg),\quad i,j=x,y

Impedance phases are in degrees measured counterclockwise from the positive real axis in the complex plane. A homogeneous earth requires E and H fields to be in phase. The in-phase condition becomes +45 degrees for Zxy and -135 degrees for Zxy for generally accepted sign conventions for E and H.

See also apparent resistivity.
